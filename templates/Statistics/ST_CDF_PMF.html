What are PMF and CDF?

The Probability Mass Function (PMF) and Cumulative Distribution Function (CDF) are ways to describe the likelihood of outcomes in a probability distribution.

They are especially useful when working with discrete random variables, like the number of heads you get in 10 coin flips.

Understanding PMF (Probability Mass Function)

What Does PMF Do?

The PMF tells you the exact probability of a specific outcome. For example:

  • What is the probability of getting exactly 5 heads when flipping a coin 10 times?
  • The PMF will calculate this probability.

Mathematically, for a binomial distribution:

P(X = k) = C(n, k) * p^k * (1 - p)^(n - k)
  • P(X = k): Probability of exactly k successes.
  • C(n, k): The number of ways to arrange k successes in n trials (a combination).
  • p: Probability of success (e.g., heads in a coin flip).
  • n: Number of trials (e.g., number of flips).

Visualizing PMF

Think of PMF as a bar chart. Each bar represents the probability of a specific outcome:

  • The height of the bar at 5 heads shows the probability of exactly 5 heads.
  • The sum of all bar heights equals 1 (since one of the outcomes must happen).

Understanding CDF (Cumulative Distribution Function)

What Does CDF Do?

The CDF gives you the cumulative probability of up to a certain number of successes.

  • What is the probability of getting 5 or fewer heads in 10 coin flips?
  • The CDF will calculate this by summing up the probabilities of all outcomes from 0 to 5 heads.

Mathematically, the CDF is:

P(X ≤ k) = Σ P(X = i) for i = 0 to k
  • P(X ≤ k): Probability of k or fewer successes.
  • Σ: Summation operator (adds up probabilities).
  • P(X = i): Probability of exactly i successes.

Visualizing CDF

Think of CDF as a step plot. Each step represents the cumulative probability up to that point:

  • The value at 5 on the x-axis shows the probability of getting 5 or fewer heads.
  • The last step (at 10) always equals 1, because it includes all possible outcomes.

PMF vs. CDF: Key Differences

  • PMF: Probability of a specific outcome (e.g., exactly 5 heads).
  • CDF: Cumulative probability up to a certain outcome (e.g., 5 or fewer heads).

If PMF is like a bar chart, CDF is like the running total of that chart.

PMF and CDF in Action

Imagine flipping a coin 10 times. Here’s what the PMF and CDF look like:

  • The PMF bar at 5 heads might show a 25% probability.
  • The CDF at 5 heads might show a 62.5% cumulative probability (adding up probabilities for 0 to 5 heads).

We can use both PMF and CDF to understand the behavior of the binomial distribution and calculate probabilities for different scenarios.

Summary of Key Takeaways

  • PMF: Tells you the probability of a specific outcome.
  • CDF: Tells you the cumulative probability up to a certain outcome.
  • Applications: Use PMF for exact outcomes and CDF for ranges of outcomes.
